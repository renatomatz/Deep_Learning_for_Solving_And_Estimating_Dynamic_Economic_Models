## Simulated method of moments

- **McFadden (1989), "A method of simulated moments for estimation of discrete response models without numerical integration,"** *Econometrica*, 57(5). The original SMM paper.
- **Pakes, Pollard (1989), "Simulation and the asymptotics of optimization estimators,"** *Econometrica*, 57(5). The companion asymptotic-theory paper for simulation-based optimization estimators.
- **Duffie, Singleton (1993), "Simulated moments estimation of Markov models of asset prices,"** *Econometrica*, 61(4). Large-sample theory in the time-series simulated-moments setting; source of the $(1+1/S)$ simulation-inflation factor used in §10.4.
- **Smith (1993), "Estimating nonlinear time-series models using simulated vector autoregressions,"** *Journal of Applied Econometrics*, 8. The companion paper that anchors much of the macro-time-series SMM literature.
- **Gourieroux, Monfort, Renault (1993), "Indirect inference,"** *Journal of Applied Econometrics*, 8. The closely-related indirect-inference framework.

## Surrogate-based estimation

- **Chen, Didisheim, Scheidegger (2026), "Deep surrogates for finance,"** *JFE*, 177. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2025.104222). Builds the inference theory for surrogate-based estimation that this lecture's notebook follows.
- **Norets (2012), "Estimation of dynamic discrete choice models using artificial neural network approximations,"** *Econometric Reviews*, 31(1). An earlier neural-network-as-surrogate-for-estimation paper.

## Beyond SMM: simulation-based inference

- **Cranmer, Brehmer, Louppe (2020), "The frontier of simulation-based inference,"** *PNAS*, 117(48). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1912789117). Survey of modern likelihood-free / simulation-based inference — the natural Bayesian neighbour of SMM discussed in §10.8.
